About

I am Kirshanthan (“Krish”) Sundararajah, a PhD candidate at the Elmore Family School of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Purdue University.

My research interests span the areas of compilers, programming languages, and high-performance computing. I am particularly interested in solving performance challenges in irregular programs. Recently, I have focused on reasoning about the correctness of composable scheduling transformations for general recursive programs.

I am fortunate enough to advised by Milind Kulkarni. I have earned my Bachelor’s in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka.
